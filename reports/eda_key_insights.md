1. Five Key Exploratory Insights   

    Insight 1: The Multi-Homing Overlap Paradox (+3pp deceleration drag) : Between 2021 and 2024, Telebirr registered over 40 million wallets, yet unique Findex account ownership grew by only 3 percentage points (46% to 49%). This highlights massive multi-homing: urban individuals who were already banked opened multiple digital wallets. The expansion added services for the already-banked rather than reaching unbanked populations.  

    Insight 2: Structural Transition from Cash-Out to Digital Circulation : For the first time, digital peer-to-peer (P2P) transfer volumes surpassed physical cash withdrawals at ATMs. This indicates that digital money is beginning to circulate directly within the economy for transactional use rather than merely serving as a digital pipeline to withdraw physical cash.  

    Insight 3: Persistent Regional & Infrastructure Segregation: Mobile money usage is highly concentrated in urban areas. This pattern aligns closely with localized 4G LTE network coverage, showing a correlation coefficient of $r = 0.84$ with active wallet usage. Infrastructure expansion remains a leading predictor of transactional activity.  

    Insight 4: The Gender Gap Gap Margin : World Bank microdata reveals a persistent gender gap of roughly 12 to 15 percentage points. This disparity is driven by lower smartphone ownership rates among women and structural barriers to acquiring formal identity documents for account registration.  

    Insight 5: Transactional Depth vs. Shallow Financial Integration: While payment adoption has grown to 35% of adults, formal credit and micro-insurance penetration remain below 3%. This suggests a market that is transactionally deep but lacks broader financial integration, limiting the long-term economic benefits of inclusion.  

2. Visual Analytics & Event Evaluation   

    Telebirr Impact (May 2021): Triggered immediate growth in registrations, but its impact on unique demand-side account ownership was delayed. This delay suggests that early adoption was concentrated among existing bank customers.  

    Safaricom & M-Pesa Entry (2022–2023): Introduced direct market competition, which helped expand agent networks in secondary urban areas and reduced cash-in/cash-out friction.  

3. Data Quality & Limitations Assessment   

    The Sparse Target Trap : The core targets (Findex Access and Usage metrics) have only five data points over 13 years (2011, 2014, 2017, 2021, and 2024). This severe sparsity rules out traditional autoregressive modeling techniques (like ARIMA or SARIMAX) without incorporating supplementary administrative datasets.  

    Definition Mismatch: Supply-side operators report "active accounts" using a 90-day transaction window, which differs from demand-side Findex surveys that measure actual personal use over a 12-month period. This reporting mismatch makes it difficult to align administrative metrics with overall inclusion targets.  

4. Modeling Hypotheses for Task 3   

    Hypothesis 1: Regulatory rollouts of foundational digital identity systems (such as the Fayda ID) will reduce access barriers, yielding a statistically significant increase in unique accounts with an estimated 6-to-12 month lag.

    Hypothesis 2: The growth of mobile wallet adoption follows an exponential decay model, where initial network effects are strong but gradually level off as the market approaches the physical infrastructure limit of mobile network coverage.
