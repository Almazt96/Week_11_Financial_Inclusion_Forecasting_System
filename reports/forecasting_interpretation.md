### 1. Forecast Projections Analysis (Baseline vs. Augmented Shocks)

Our event-augmented model predicts that Ethiopia is on track for steady financial inclusion growth. [cite_start]This growth is driven by the gradual impact of major infrastructure and regulatory milestones from the 2021–2024 period[cite: 416, 543, 673].

#### Key Projection Highlights
* [cite_start]**Access (Account Ownership):** Under the **Base Scenario**, account ownership is projected to reach **59.4%** by late 2026, putting Ethiopia on track to hit the NFIS-II **60% target** by early 2027[cite: 662, 717].
* **Usage (Digital Payments):** Digital payment adoption is expected to rise to **51.2%** by 2027. This growth reflects the transition of rural users from cash to digital platforms, supported by expanding merchant and agent networks.

### 2. Event-Driven Growth Drivers

The model identifies several key events as the primary drivers of this growth:

1. [cite_start]**National Fayda Digital ID Scale-Out (High Impact on Access):** Integrating the Fayda digital identity system with mobile money onboarding reduces e-KYC compliance hurdles[cite: 477, 549]. [cite_start]By eliminating the need for traditional physical paper documents, this event is estimated to add a **+6 percentage point boost** to unique account registrations by 2027[cite: 549].
2. [cite_start]**Safaricom and M-Pesa Agent Expansion (High Impact on Usage):** The expansion of agent networks in secondary cities reduces cash-in/cash-out friction[cite: 416, 597, 598]. [cite_start]This competitive pressure is estimated to add a **+9 percentage point boost** to digital payment adoption by 2027[cite: 416, 549, 597].

### 3. Scenario Analysis Pathways

* [cite_start]**Optimistic Scenario (High-Velocity Growth):** Assumes full regulatory interoperability, swift Fayda ID integration, and consistent mobile network expansion[cite: 477, 543]. [cite_start]In this pathway, Account Ownership reaches **64.6%** by 2026, and Digital Payment Usage climbs to **59.2%** by 2027[cite: 662, 674].
* [cite_start]**Pessimistic Scenario (Stagnation Pathway):** Assumes slow implementation of e-KYC rules, persistent infrastructure or network reliability issues, and low smartphone adoption[cite: 543, 581]. [cite_start]This scenario limits Account Ownership growth to **52.2%** and leaves Digital Payment Usage at **39.4%** by 2027, missing the national policy targets[cite: 662, 674].

### 4. Methodological Assumptions, Limitations & Uncertainties

#### Key Assumptions
* **Interoperability:** Assumes payment systems remain fully interoperable across all banking platforms and mobile wallets.
* **Agent Expansion:** Assumes mobile money operators face no significant regulatory barriers to expanding their agent networks in rural regions.

#### Primary Uncertainties
* [cite_start]**The Sparse-Data Limitation:** Since we are relying on only five historical Findex points, our trend estimates are highly sensitive to any revisions or anomalies in the historical data[cite: 667, 678].
* **Registration vs. Active Usage:** Supply-side account registration metrics must be discounted to accurately estimate unique, active demand-side usage, as many registered accounts remain inactive or represent duplicate users.
